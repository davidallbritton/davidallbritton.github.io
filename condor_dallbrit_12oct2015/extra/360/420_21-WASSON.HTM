The Wason selection task (Wason & Johnson-Laird, 1972)

      E F 4 7
  • Each card has a letter on one side, a number on the other.
  • Hypothesis: "If a card has a vowel on one side, it has an even number on the other."

    Correct answer: Turn over "E" and "7"

    Typical error: Turn over "E" and "4"

Why do people make this error? The matching hypothesis

  • Subjects tend to match terms in the hypothesis ("vowel", "even number") and matching them to states of affairs in the data (the "E" and "4" cards).
  • Evidence for the matching hypothesis:
    • Change hypothesis to "If a card has a vowel on one side, it does not have an even number on the other"
    • Now the terms mentioned in the hypothesis correspond to the correct cards to turn over. (In other words, using a matching strategy to test the hypothesis will not lead to errors as it did in the original task).
    • Subjects make far fewer errors.
