Evidence for
Signal Detection Theory
• Swets, Tannar, & Birdsall, 1961
– 4 choice detection task (tones A, B, C, D)
– After an incorrect answer, answered again
– If the participants had no information about which tone
it was when they were wrong, should be at chance on
subsequent guesses
– Results: after a missed guess, they were above chance
on the next guess
– Therefore, the threshold is a continuous function (as
Signal Detection Theory predicts), not a step function.
