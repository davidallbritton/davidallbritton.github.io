The inflation rate is defined to be the percentage change in the annual CPI.  For example, the CPI in 1998 was 163.0 while the CPI in 1999 was 166.6.   The inflation rate for 1999 was therefore (166.6-163.0)/163.0 or 2.2%.  The inflation rate is important for many reasons.  High inflation means that the money we earn and have saved up in the past is worth less. Interest rates for houses, cars, and credit cards rise if the inflation rate is high. For a variety of reasons, stock prices tend to fall even on hints of higher inflation. Increases for many government entitlement programs are tied to the inflation rate.
