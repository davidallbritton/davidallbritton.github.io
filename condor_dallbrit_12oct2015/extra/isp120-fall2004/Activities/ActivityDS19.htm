1.  Open the file AutotheftRates.xls which contains data on the autotheft rate for all fifty states in 2000.  We are interested in comparing states which are west of the Mississippi River (indicated with a W) and those that are east of the Mississippi (indicated with a E).

a.  Calculate the mean, range, and standard deviation for the eastern states. (Recall the range is difference between the maximum and minimum and the standard deviation is calculated using =STDEV(dataseries)).

b. Calculate the mean, range, and standard deviation for the western states.

c. What does the standard deviation measure in general?

d.  In one or two sentences describe the difference in both the mean and the spread between the eastern and western states.

e. While there is no hard and fast rule, almost every one will agree that if a data point lies more than  two standard deviations from the mean, it can be called an outlier.  Are there any outliers among the eastern states?  How about among the western states?

f. One state is truly an outlier, as you saw in part e.  Calculate the mean for the western states omitting that one state.  How does the mean for the western states now compare to the mean for the eastern states?  Why was there such a dramatic change?
