Group Activity 3: Relative Risk

1.  A 1999 study by the Harvard Medical School (Journal of the National Cancer Institute, February 17, 1999) reviewed 72 previous studies on the effect of tomatoes on cancer.  The question the study addressed was whether high consumers of tomatoes and tomato products have a lower risk of various cancers. Consider the following table, which shows the incidence of oral cancer for a group of people who ate an average of one tomato a day and another group of people who ate fewer than three tomatoes per week.

 

  No oral cancer Oral cancer
Equivalent of one tomato per day 191 9
Fewer than the equivalent of three tomatoes per week 164 16

a.  What is the risk of oral cancer for the two groups?

b.  What is the relative risk for these two groups?  Express your answer clearly in a sentence.

c. What is the increased risk of oral cancer for those who eat fewer than three tomatoes per week? Express your answer clearly in a sentence.

2. A study on the effect of coffee on gall stones (Journal of the American Medical Association, June 9, 1999) included the following data shown below:

  Gall stone disease No gall stone disease
No caffeinated coffee 385 14068
More than four cups of caffeinated coffee per day 91 4806

a. What is the risk of gall stone disease for the two groups? 

b.  What is the relative risk for these two groups.  Express your answer clearly in a sentence. Is there a greater risk in drinking coffee or not drinking coffee?

c. What is the decreased risk of gall stone disease for those who drink a great deal of coffee versus those who drink no caffeinated coffee? Express your answer clearly in a sentence.
