Relative Risk (RR)

Consider a population followed prospectively as shown in Table 1. The risk of developing disease among those with a risk factor is A/(A + B). Similarly, the risk of developing disease among those without the risk factor is C/(C + D). The risk for those with the risk factor relative to those without the risk factor is the ratio of the two risks: 

                          A/(A + B)
                   RR =   _________
                          C/(C + D)

Although (RR) could be calculated for a prospective study, often it is not.
