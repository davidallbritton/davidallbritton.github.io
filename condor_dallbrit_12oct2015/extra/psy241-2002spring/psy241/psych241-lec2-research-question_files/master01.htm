Agreement: effect will be present when the cause is present.  Find a single circumstance that is common to all occurrences of an event. Example:  genetic defect (extra chromosome) and downs syndrome
Difference:  effect will be absent when cause is absent.  Find a single difference between occurrence and absence of an event. 
Example:  solar eclipse always occurs during new moon
Conc. Variation: cause covaries with the degree of the event – correlation.  Example: smoking and lung cancer.
Other expl. Ruled out: example red cars and speeding tickets.
How to rule out other explanations?  Good research design that controls extraneous variables.
Conv. Operations:  STM capacity defined as forward digit span, backwards digit span, number of words recalled…
From theory:  theory: evolution favors different reproductive strategies for males than females; males should seek young attractive partners (indicators of fertility).  Females should seek dominant, resource-laden males (better able to protect and provide for offspring).  Research question that follows from this theory: Do personals ads reflect these different strategies?
Conflicting results:  “horse raced past the barn fell” – context could prevent garden paths in self-paced reading, but not in eye-tracking studies.  Hypothesis: context does not affect initial parse (syntactic structure) of sentence, but does affect ease of reanalysis if the initial parse is wrong. Case studies: HM’s hipocampus removal and amnesia; led to research using neuroimaging examining role of hipocampus in memory formation Personal experience:  example- suspense.  Theories based on “surprise” as source of suspense did not explain how you can experience suspense when you already know the outcome.
