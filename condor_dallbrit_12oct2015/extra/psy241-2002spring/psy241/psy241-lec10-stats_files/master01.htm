Desc stats: describe our sample of data
Inf stats: use our sample to draw conclusions about the population it came from
Example:  opinion poll:  desc: % supporting bush/gore
infer: “margin of error” – says that we are 95% confident that the *real* (population) percentage is within a certain range based on our sample.
Example: ask class how many pets they have had total.  Put in an spss data file.  This is the population (the whole class) •Take a random sample of 5 from the data file (have a student provide 5 random subject numbers from a random number table) and calculate the mean
•Take 2 more random samples
•Ask what they think the population mean is (between the sample means somewhere)
•Calculate the population mean in spss.
•Note that none of the sample means were the same as the population mean
•Remind them about the normal distribution; note that a distribution of sample means (the sampling distribution of the mean) is normally distributed •Note that the difference between 2 means is also normally distributed; this fact underlies the t-test (sort of)
Means:   2.6 vs 5.1 (right foot)
