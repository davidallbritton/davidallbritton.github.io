Hermeneutical approach:  look for *reasons* (interpretations) rather than *causes*
A non-exp study is one in which the experimenter does not have enough control to determine who is in what condition at what time.
Surveys: not covered here.  Main issues are sample selection (unbiased), reactive measures (does the form of the survey change the answers), reliability & validity.
Nat obs: watching without affecting what happens
Part obs: example, Hunter Thompson’s book Hells Angels, while really journalism and not research, has also been regarded as a valuable piece of participant-observer sociological research on what was, at the time, a group that outsiders knew little about (largely because of their outlaw status)
Physical trace: unobtrusive measure that makes use of physical evidence of behavior.  Examples: examining wear patterns on books to see which are most read; graffiti
Hermeneutic: emphasizing interpretation and understanding rather than cause and effect
Objectivity: participant often begins to sympathize with group, even joins
Reactivity: participant’s presence can change the group
Privacy and consent: ethical dilemma, especially for disguised participation.
(discuss: is it ok to pretend to join a group for purposes of research?  What justifies doing away with informed consent, debriefing, etc.?  What about the privacy of other group members?)
FACS: facial action coding scheme (Ekman). 
-Identified “action units” indiv facial muscles
-catalogued the facial appearance associated with use of each action unit.
-Were then able to analyze facial expressions and identify action units associated with different emotions

-Memory limitations: observer will forget some things if not noted immediately -Observer bias: systematic recording of observed behavior can reduce observer bias

-Variety of measures:  increase construct validity of research; reduce (potentially) observer bias
Manifest: more objective; counting certain words, etc.
More reliable (b/c it is objective), but less valid sometimes (because of lexical ambiguity, difference between surface features and deep structure meaning, etc)
Latent: coding for meaning, etc.
** The content analysis will usually define the IVs for the study, and perhaps the DVs as well **
May not ask what you need to know:  using census records to examine relationship of income to domestic violence.  No info on domestic violence in census probably.
Bias: example- using medical records to determine correlates of mental illness. Problem: doctors may list a more serious diagnosis so that the HMO will provide treatment.
