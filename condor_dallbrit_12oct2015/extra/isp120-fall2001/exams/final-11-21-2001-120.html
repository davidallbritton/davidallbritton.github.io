1.  Open the file Greenhouse.xls, which contains data on three "greenhouse" gases, carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide, that were present in the atmosphere before the industrial revolution.  These gases are called greenhouse gases because in the atmosphere they act like glass in a greenhouse, allowing sunlight to pass through but preventing reflected infrared radiation from escaping back to space.  Increases in the concentrations of these gases can be safely ascribed to human activity.  Calculate the percentage change in the concentration of each gas, making sure to express the answer correctly in percent to one decimal place (e.g. "35.1%").  Paste the resulting table into your Word document.

2. Open the file Tuberculosis_By_State96.xls, which contains data on tuberculosis in each of the fifty states in 1996.

a) Which three states had the highest numbers of tuberculosis cases in 1996? Which three had the lowest? (Style tip: A good style for answering a question such as this is to give the state with the number in parentheses right after it: Missouri (204).)

b) Which three states had the highest tuberculosis rates (in cases per 1000 population)?

c) Using Excel's map tool, make a shaded map of the tuberculosis rate by state.   Paste your map into your Word document and add a short well-written paragraph describing the geographic distribution of the tuberculosis. What patterns do you see? Are there any outliers?

d) Open the file Tuberculosis95-96.xls.   Find the percentage change in tuberculosis cases for each state as well as for the whole country. In your Word document include your answers to the following questions: i)  Did the majority of states have a  percentage increase? ii) What was the percentage change for Illinois?  iii) What was the percentage change for the US?

3. In 1997, the population of Mexico was 94,275,000 and the population of the United States was 267,839,000.  By what percent is the United States larger than Mexico?

4. Estimate the amount of money spent each year in the US on pet food.

5.

a)     In midyear 1998, the population of Mexico was 98,553,000.  Mexico is growing at 1.9% annually. Assuming that the trend continues, use an exponential model to predict the population of Mexico in midyear 2005.  Include you table in your Word document.

b)    How much confidence do you have in your prediction?  What factors could affect the growth rate?

6. Open the file Rural.xls which contains decennial census data on the percentage of the U.S. population living in rural areas from 1850 to 1990.

a) Create an XY scatterplot (not connected) of the percent living in rural areas from 1850 to 1990. Add a linear model (trendline)  to your graph, including the equation and R2 value.  Paste the graph into your Word document.

b) In a short well-written paragraph, carefully describe the graph.  (Remember concepts such as global and local maxima and minima, increasing and decreasing behavior, and overall trend.)

c) Using the equation, predict the percentage of the US population living in rural areas in the year 2000.  How confident are you regarding this prediction? Why?

d) Using the equation, determine when the percentage of the US population living in rural areas will be 0.  How confident are you regarding this prediction? Cite at least two factors that could affect the prediction.

7.  State whether each of the following relationships is most likely to be described by a linear model, an exponential model, or neither, and explain why that is the most likely of the three choices in that case.

a)     The relationship between the number of years $1000 is left in a savings account and the balance of the account.

b)    The relationship between wage-earners’ weights and their annual incomes.

c)     The relationship between the number of miles driven and the amount of gasoline used.

d)    The relationship between the number of bacteria and the amount of time that has passed, assuming that there is an unlimited nutrient supply for the bacteria.
