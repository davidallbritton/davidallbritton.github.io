  1. Open the file HIV_Infections.xls. This file contains World Health Organization data on the countries with the highest numbers of adult HIV infections in 1994.
    1. Determine the three countries that have the highest number of HIV infections.
    2. In the next column of the table, calculate the percentage of adults for each country who are infected with HIV. This percentage is referred to as the adult HIV infection rate. Paste this table into your Word document.
    3. Determine the three countries that have the highest infection rate. Of the countries on the entire list, which country has the lowest infection rate?
    4. In a short well-written paragraph, explain why the list of countries with the highest number of HIV infections differs from the list of those that have the highest infection rate.

  2. Estimate the number of refrigerators sold in the US in 1998. Hint

3.               a)  In 1998, the population of the US was approximately 270 million and the population of Mexico was 98.6 million.  By how many more percent was the population of the US larger than the population of Mexico?

b) If the population of Mexico grew by 6.2% from 1998 to 2001, what would the population of Mexico be in 2001?

4.  Because of the sudden drop in travel and tourism, many cast members in Broadway shows have agreed to take a 25% pay cut to help keep the shows open.  Suppose that they were to be given a 30% pay increase once ticket sales improve.  By what percentage would an actor’s resulting salary have increased or decreased from what it originally was before the pay cut?
