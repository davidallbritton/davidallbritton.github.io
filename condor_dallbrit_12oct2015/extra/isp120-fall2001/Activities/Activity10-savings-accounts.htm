Savings Accounts - Activity 13

1.   Suppose that you deposit $500 in a bank that offers an APR of 6.0% compounded monthly.

        a.  What is your account balance after one year?
        b.  What is your account balance after 10 years?
        c.  What is the annual percentage yield for this account?

2.  Suppose that you deposit $800 in a bank that offers an APR of 7.25% compounded quarterly.

        a.  What is your account balance after one year?
        b.  What is your account balance after 10 years?
        c.  What is the annual percentage yield for this account?

3.  Compare the accumulated balance in three accounts that all start with an initial deposit of $1000. All three accounts have an APR of 5.5%, but the first account compounds interest annually, the second account compounds interest quarterly while the third account compounds interest monthly. Make a table that shows the accumulated balance in all three accounts for the first ten years.

        a.  Paste this chart in your Word document.
        b.  Write a paragraph discussing the differences between the accounts. Which account is the largest? smallest?
        c.  Calculate the APY for each account and type your answers in your Word document.
        d.  Do you want an account that compounds interest annually, quarterly or monthly?

4. Suppose you start saving today for a $5,000 house payment that you plan to make in 5 years. Assume you make no deposits into the account after your initial deposit. In order to reach your $5,000 goal, how much will you need to deposit in each account described?

a. An account with annual compounding and an APR of 9%.

b. An account with quarterly compounding and an APR of 9%.

c. An account with monthly compounding and an APR of 9%.
