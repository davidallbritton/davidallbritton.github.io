Savings Accounts - Activity 11

1.  Suppose that you invest $1000 in an account that earns simple interest at an annual rate of 6% per year. At the same time your friend invests $1000 in a savings account with annual compounding at a rate of 6% per year. Make a spreadsheet which includes years 0 through 25 and calculates the amount earned each year. The first column should include numbers 0 through 25. The second should begin with an investment of $1000 and increase with simple interest through year 25. The third column should increase with compound interest through year 25. Label each column with a descriptive heading. Assuming that neither of you withdraws any money, compare how much you each will have in your accounts after twenty-five years. Use all three options we learned in class to calculate the savings account balance where the interest compounds annually. 

a.  Copy years 20 through 25 in your Word document.
b.  Which account (simple or compound) has the most money after 25 years?

2.   Suppose that you deposit $500 in a bank that offers an APR of 6.0% compounded annually. Use all three options we learned in class.

        a.  What is your account balance after one year?
        b.  What is your account balance after 10 years?
        c.  What is the annual percentage yield for this account?

3.  Suppose that you deposit $800 in a bank that offers an APR of 7.25% compounded annually.  Use all three options we learned in class.

        a.  What is your account balance after one year?
        b.  What is your account balance after 10 years?
        c.  What is the annual percentage yield for this account?

4. How long will it take your money to triple at an APR of 8% compounded annually?

5. How long will it take your money to grow by 50% at an APR of 7% compounded annually?
