Activity 19: Scaling of Graphs

All group activities must include a signed statement that all members of your group participated fully in the assignment. To start you might want to open a new Word document entitled something like "Group Activity 16" and put the names of your group members at the top.

1. Open the file AIDS90-95.xls, which gives data on the number of deaths in the US due to AIDS.

a. Create an XY graph of the data and include it in your Word document.

b. Desribe the graph in a well written paragraph.

c. Double click on the y-axis.  Click on the "Scale" tab and set the minimum equal to 29,000 and maximum equal to 45,000.  Paste this chart in your Word document.

d. Describe the new graph in a well-written paragraph.

e. What effect does the scaling of y-axis have on the interpretation of the data? Which graph would you use to make a graphical representation of the data?

2. Open the file CableRates.xls, which gives the average rates for cable TV in the US from 1980 to 1997.

a. By how many percent did cable rates rise from 1980 to 1997?

b. Make a graph of the data and include it in your Word document.

c. If you were writing an article about cable rates, would you use the figure you calculated in a. and the graph you just made? Why?

d. Convert the prices into 1998 dollars (do you remember how to do that?  Add the CPI values in the next column and then in the next column do the conversion....). Make a graph of the cable rates in 1998 dollars and paste it into your Word document. 

e. Double click on the y-axis.  Click on the "Scale" tab and set the minimum equal to 13 and maximum equal to 28.  Paste this chart in your Word document.

f. By how many percent have cable rates (in constant 1998 dollars) risen?

f. If you were writing an article and you wanted to emphasize that cable prices are high, which graph would you use?

g. In a short paragraph, speculate why cable rates have risen so much faster than inflation.
