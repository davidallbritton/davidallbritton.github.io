Activity 18: Correlation between Global Temperatures and Carbon Dioxide

Quantitative Reasoning

All group activities must include a statement signed by all group members asserting that all members of your group participated fully in the assignment. To start you might want to open a new Word document entitled something like "Group Activity 18" and put the names of your group members at the top.

1. a. Open the file GlobalTemp1866-1995.xls. By what percentage have global temperatures risen between the two years 1866 and 1995?

b. Make a plot of the global temperature data. Describe the graph in a few well written sentences.  Based on this graph can one definitively conclude that global warming is occurring? Why or why not?

2. a. Now open the file VostokIceCoreData.xls, which contains data taken from deep ice cores in Vostok, Antarctica.  What are the units for time in this data?

b. Make a plot of the Vostok CO2 data.  (Change the scale on the vertical axis for this graph to a minimum of 180 and a maximum of 375.)  Make a separate graph of the Vostok temperature data.  Place them next to each other in your Word document.   From these graphs is there any relationship between CO2 levels and Vostok temperatures? What differences do you see?
c. Study the last 60 thousand years in a little more detail. Some climatologists have asserted that rising CO2 levels have helped prevent a cooling period on earth in the last 1000 years. Does the data support this theory?

d. Make a scatterplot (not connected) with the the Vostok CO2 readings on the x-axis and the Vostok temperature data on the other.  Add a linear trendline to the data.  Is there a correlation between the CO2 readings and the temperatures? If so, how strong is the correlation?

e. Is there an outlier in the graph you made in d?  What year does it come from?  Remove the point and find the R2 value without it.  To what extent does the correlation improve?

e. Based on the global temperature and Vostok data, is there some evidence that global warming is occurring? Explain your reasoning.
