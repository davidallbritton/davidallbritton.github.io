a. The units that carbon dioxide measurements use are "ppmv" or parts per million by volume.  The article mentions these units but doesn't explain them. c. Open the file MaunaLoa.xls, the file that contains the famous Keeling data.  Make a plot (XY-Scatter, connected) of the monthly averages with the y-axis scale from 0 to 400. Adjust the x-axis so that it begins with 1/58; i.e.right click on the x-axis, choose "Format axis", choose the scale tab and type and 1/58 in the minimum box.  Include the graph  in your Word document.

d. Now adjust the y-axis scale so that it runs from 300 ppmv to 370 ppmv.   Include this graph in your Word document.  Briefly discuss the different impressions made by the graphs in c and d due to scaling. 

e.  In a short well-written paragraph, describe the curve.

g. What causes the annual fluctuations in the CO2 levels? Explain your reasoning.

h. Using the annual average data, calculate the percentage increase in CO2 concentration from 1959 to 1999. (This is the percentage increase for the entire period 1959 to 1999.)
