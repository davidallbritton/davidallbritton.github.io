1. The sales tax on most items in the state of Illinois is 8.75%.  (It is 2% on food.)

2. Open the file WorldUrbanPopulations.xls. This file contains data from the World Bank on the city populations of most of the countries in the world.  (A small number of countries whose data is incomplete are omitted for simplicity.) We would like to study the extent to which people live in cities internationally.   A person is considered to be an city dweller if he or she lives in a city, a suburb of a city, or a town with a population of 2,500 or more.  A "city" is a municipality with a population of more than 50,000 and with a population density greater than 1,000 people per square mile. A country is said to be "urbanized" if a large fraction of its population lives in urban areas.

  1. Determine the three countries had the highest city populations in 1996 and write their names and their city populations in your Word document. Use a complete sentence.
  2. Explain in a short well-written paragraph why the countries with the largest city populations are not necessarily the most urbanized.
  3. Add a column to your Excel spreadsheet that contains the percent of each country that lives in an urban area.  Sort the data on this column.  After sorting, paste a table containing the top ten most urbanized countries into your Word document.
  4. Make a shaded map showing the percent of urbanization internationally (use all countries).  Paste it into your Word document and in a short well-written paragraph describe the geographic patterns.  Discuss outliers, if any.
  5. What other data would you seek if you wanted to understand the extent to which people around the world live in cities?
