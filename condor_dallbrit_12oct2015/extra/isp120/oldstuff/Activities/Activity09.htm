2.  Stores such as Sam's Club and Price-Costco charge an annual membership fee of approximately $30.  As a member you can purchase products at lower prices, but typically in larger quantities than one buys at ordinary stores. Savings vary from product to product, but for this question, let us assume you can save $10 for every $100 dollars you spend at one of these stores compared to shopping elsewhere.

a.  Suppose you spend $100 dollars in one year at such a store.  Do you come out ahead? How much do you save (or lose?) relative to not having membership and paying full price?

b. How much do you have to spend at the store for it to be worthwhile for you to buy membership?

c. The amount you save (or lose) by buying membership is a linear function of how much you spend.  Explain why.

d. Write a linear equation for your savings as a function of how much you spend at the store.  Since your equation will involve variables, explicitly identify the meaning of each variable.  (E.g.  "Let S be the amount of savings.")

e. What other factors would you consider when deciding to become a member of Sam's Club or Price-Costco?

3. In 1969, 6.6% of all state legislators across the US were female.  In 1997, 25.4% were female.  The linear function that passes through the points (1969,6.6%) and (1997,25.4%) is a fairly good mathematical model for the increase in the percentage of female state legislators.

a. Find the equation for this model.

b. Use the equation to give a prediction for the percentage of female state legislator in 2010.

c. Use the model to predict when the percentage of female legislators might reach 50%.
