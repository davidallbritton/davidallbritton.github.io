Activity 18: Introduction to Categorical Data Analysis

In this activity you will begin to learn how to use Excel to analyze categorical data.   "Categorical data analysis" refers to the process of  grouping data and summarizing the data for various groupings so as to make inferences.  The classic example of this process is analyzing a survey, but one also encounters this same process in analyzing purchase data in stores and in "clickstream" data on the internet.  Experience working with such data considerably expands your data analysis capability.

We will analyze complex survey data about the Chicago area.  Every year, the Metropolitan Chicago Information Center, an independent, not-for-profit research organization, conducts a detailed random survey of approximately 3000 individuals in the Chicago metropolitan area.  The survey covers a wide gamut of public policy and quality of life issues: transportation, leisure, crime, taxation, housing, health, education and much more.  This information is used by a wide variety of not-for-profit organizations, community groups, government agencies, universities, hospitals, and the business community in assessing needs, designing programs, and allocating resources.
