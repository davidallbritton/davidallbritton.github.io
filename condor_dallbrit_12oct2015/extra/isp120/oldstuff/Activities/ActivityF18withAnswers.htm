The more frequent the compounding, the higher the annual yield, so quarterly is higher than annually, and monthly is higher than quarterly.  However also note that there is a smaller increase moving from annually to quarterly than moving from quarterly to monthly.  You would expect the yield to increase even more going from monthly to daily, but in fact, it doesn't increase very much (with 365 days, it goes to 1056.54, about 13 cents more on $1000).   This aspect of compounding is surprising.  There are mathematical reasons for it, but it is still unexpected.
