Lab 1.1 – Mapping the Blind Spot

Questions asked on screen during the blind spot lab exercise (MEL Lab 1.1)

Write down your answers on this sheet before you press the y/n keys to answer the questions on the screen.  You will answer the questions on this sheet in the first part of the lab exercise.  Then the next part of the exercise will map your blind spot.  At the end of that part, you will write down answers to questions in your lab workbook.

Be sure you write down all your answers as you go, because this lab exercise does not store any data on disk – you only have one chance to write it down when it appears on the screen.

The first three questions are a test of the retino-cortical mapping theory of how the blind spot is filled in.

  1. Do the squares above and below seem to move closer together?
    [2 squares, above and below the square in the blind spot]
  2. Does the vertical line seem to shorten?
    [A vertical line with the blind spot at its center]
  3. Do the parallel lines seem to converge as they approach the blind spot? 
    [2 parallel lines to the right and left of the square that is in the blind spot]

The next 5 displays have to do with how the blind spot is filled in.

  1. Has the empty space been colored in?
    [Large white circle with a black rectangle in the blind spot]
  2. Has the empty space been filled in with “t”s? 
    [an array of rows of the letter “t” with an empty rectangle in the center in the blind spot]
  3.  [A box in the center of a vertical line; while it is in the blind spot, press the spacebar and it disappears from the screen.  Keep your eyes still for a couple of seconds after pressing the spacebar, then open your eyes.] 
    Did you see the box disappear when you opened your left eye?
  4. [Using the arrow keys, move a vertical line up and down through the blind spot.]  Was the line seen as complete when it extended all the way through your blind spot?

 

After completing the lab exercise, answer the questions in the MEL Lab workbook that accompany Lab 1.1, and turn in your answers along with the map of your blind spot.  This is the only lab exercise that you will not have to do in the form of an APA style research paper – simply answer the questions in well-written paragraphs.
