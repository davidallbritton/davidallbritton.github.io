  1.  Estimate the following.  As usual, your estimate should start with some basic, commonly accepted facts or quantities.  It should include the reasoning process by which you obtain your estimate, and the estimate should be expressed to an appropriate degree of precision (generally not too many decimal places!)  Write your answer in the form of a well-written paragraph.

a. The number of school children (K-12) in the US
b. The number of US workers who are in unions

2. Open the file USTobacco.xls which contains data on the total amount of tobacco produced in the US.

a. Make an XY scatter plot of the data. Include it in your document.

b. In a well written paragraph describe the graph. Remember concepts such as global maxima and minima, increasing, decreasing, etc.

c. Add a linear trendline to your plot, including the equation and the R-squared value. Include it in your document.

d. Use the equation in c. to predict the amount of tobacco produced in 1992.

e. How much confidence do you have in your prediction? Why?

3. Open the file Smoking.xls which contains data on the percent of the US population that smokes cigarettes.

a. Graph the data (do not connect the dots) and make three linear models (one for total, one for males, one for females).  Paste the resulting chart into your Word document.

b. Using your models, predict the percentage of the population (total, male, female) that smoke in 2001. How much faith do you have in your prediction?

c. Use your model to estimate how what percentage of the total population smoked in 1965.  How much faith do you have in your prediction?

d. Use your model to estimate when 100% of the US population smoked.  How much faith do you have in your prediction?

Open the file StrontiumAluminum.xls which contains data from a study of soils in Japan.  The study addresses the question: can the concentration of strontium be predicted reliably by determining the concentration of aluminum?  It is considerably less expensive to determine the concentration of aluminum in a sample of soil.

a) Create a XY scatterplot of the strontium level and the aluminum level.  Add a linear trendline to your graph, including the equation for the line and R2 value.  Paste the resulting graph into your Word document.

b) In a short, well-written paragraph describe what your findings indicate about the relationship between strontium and aluminum levels in the soil.

c) Using the equation, predict the level of strontium if the aluminum level is 260.  How confident are you in your prediction? Why?

d) Using the equation, predict at what  level of aluminum the strontium level would be 0.  How confident are you in your prediction? Why?
