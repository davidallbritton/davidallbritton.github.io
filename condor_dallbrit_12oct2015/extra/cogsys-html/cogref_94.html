3.3.3 CIRCEXT

Syntax: CIRCEXT radius

The circle extension, CIRCEXT is even simpler to use than SQUAREXT, since it takes only one argument, the radius, and has no position parameters (what makes it more complicated than SQUAREXT is it's implementation, see section CIRCEXT Implementation). CIRCEXT simply draws a white circle centered on a black background.

In the complete testlist below, each %I command creates a circle with a different radius.

 
%X[circext.cxr]
%M2%C2
%I[1,10]
%I[1,20]
%I[1,30]
%I[1,50]
#B[1,20]#R
#B[2,20]#R
#B[3,20]#R
#B[4,20]#R
