The Logic is Always the Same:
1.Assume nothing is going on (assume H0)
2.Calculate a test statistic (Chi-square, t, F)
3.How often would you get a value this large for the test statistic when H0 is true? (In other words, calculate p)
4.If p <  .05, reject the null hypothesis and conclude that something is going on (H1)
5.If p > .05, do not conclude anything.
