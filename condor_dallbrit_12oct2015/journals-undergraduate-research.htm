Journals for Undergraduate Research

 

If you are thinking about publishing your research, the first step you should take as a student is to speak to a faculty advisor, who will be familiar with the professional journals that might be appropriate outlets for your work.  You may also want to consider publishing your work in a journal specifically dedicated to student research, such as those listed below.  Your faculty advisor can help you weigh the pros and cons of the options available to you.

 

If you find any errors or outdated information in this list, or if you would like to suggest an additional entry, please email me, dallbrit at depaul dot edu, with your suggestion.

 

 

PRINTED JOURNALS SPECIFICALLY FOR STUDENT RESEARCH

 

 

Journal of Psychological Inquiry

  • http://puffin.creighton.edu/psy/journal/
  • The student must be from a university that sponsors the journal, or must have presented the work at specific conferences in Kansas, Missouri, and Arkansas.  See their web page for details.

 

Journal of Psychology and the Behavioral Sciences (JPBS)

  • http://view.fdu.edu/default.aspx?id=784
  • The student must be the first author

 

Modern Psychological Studies

  • http://www.utc.edu/Academic/Psychology/ModernPsychologicalStudies.php
  • (Founded 1992)

 

Psi Chi Journal of Undergraduate Research

  • http://www.psichi.org/pubs/journal/
  • The first author must be an undergraduate member of Psi Chi
